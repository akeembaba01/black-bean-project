Tapping into a Growing Market

The European alternative protein market is on a significant upward trajectory, driven by a strategic push for protein self-sufficiency. This creates a powerful, policy-backed demand for innovative solutions like black beans, moving beyond pure market forces and positioning the project for long-term success.

The Power of the Black Bean

Black beans offer a compelling nutritional profile and are recognized as climate-resilient crops. However, they face challenges from abiotic stresses, and their full potential must be realized through genetic advancements and sustainable practices.

Navigating EU Regulations

Pathway: Notification

Process: Notify via the Feed Materials Register for immediate market entry, subject to potential post-market review.

Key Consideration: Requires robust data proving anti-nutritional factors are safely mitigated through processing.

Pathway: Authorization

Process: Requires submission of a comprehensive safety dossier to the European Commission, followed by a scientific assessment by EFSA.

Timeline: Averages 2.5+ years, presenting a significant delay to market entry that must be strategically planned for.

A Structured Approach: Work Packages

The project is organized into five key Work Packages (WPs), each with specific objectives, activities, and deliverables to ensure a systematic and impactful approach from foundational research to market readiness and policy influence.

WP1: Project Coordination and Management

Objective: To ensure efficient, ethical, and compliant management, fostering effective collaboration among all partners and ensuring timely delivery of objectives.

  • Establish and maintain robust consortium operating procedures.
  • Conduct day-to-day project coordination and financial management.
  • Organize regular consortium meetings and internal workshops.
  • Develop and update a comprehensive Data Management Plan (DMP).
  • Manage intellectual property rights (IPR) effectively.

WP2: Enhancing Black Bean Genetic Resources

Objective: To broaden the genetic basis of black beans, develop climate-resilient varieties, and improve breeding methodologies.

  • Characterize existing black bean germplasm for stress tolerance and nutritional traits.
  • Implement targeted breeding programs for climate-resilient varieties.
  • Explore advanced genetic tools like transformation and gene editing.
  • Conduct multi-location field trials to validate performance.

WP3: Sustainable Cultivation Practices

Objective: To develop and validate sustainable black bean cultivation practices that enhance environmental performance and farmer resilience.

  • Assess ecological farming approaches across Europe.
  • Develop and test innovative conservation agriculture and agroforestry practices.
  • Quantify the environmental benefits and ecosystem services.
  • Co-develop decision-support tools and practical guidelines for farmers.

WP4: Value Chain Development and Nutritional Enhancement (Animal Feed-Specific)

Objective: To optimize the black bean value chain, from post-harvest to the feed sector, enhancing its nutritional benefits, exploring new market opportunities, and ensuring safety for animals.

  • Develop processes to turn black beans into cost-effective and palatable animal feed components.
  • Conduct nutritional and bioavailability studies to prove the black bean protein is an effective substitute for existing feed sources.
  • Establish the safe inclusion rates of black bean-derived products in various livestock diets (e.g., poultry, pigs, cattle).
  • Assess the impact on animal performance, health, and meat/milk quality.
  • Analyze the economic viability for EU feed manufacturers.

WP5: Dissemination and Stakeholder Engagement

Objective: To ensure effective communication, knowledge transfer, and stakeholder engagement to maximize impact and long-term sustainability.

  • Develop and execute a comprehensive communication and outreach strategy.
  • Establish robust communication channels, including a website and social media.
  • Organize workshops, training sessions, and field days for farmers.
  • Co-develop evidence-based policy recommendations.
